Indian Sign Language Infrastructure

This is MIRA

Real-time spoken and written language, converted into Indian Sign Language.

MIRA is built for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing individuals. It restructures meaning — not just words — into sign language produced by a 3D signing avatar. No cameras. No captions. No guessing.

MIRA produces sign language — it does not display captions, recognize gestures via camera, or replace human interpreters. It is a tool, not a substitute.

Clarity First

What MIRA Is — And Isn't

Understanding what MIRA does starts with understanding what it deliberately does not do.

MIRA does

  • ✓ Convert voice or text into structured Indian Sign Language
  • ✓ Restructure meaning into sign-friendly grammar — not word-for-word translation
  • ✓ Produce signs via a 3D avatar with pre-authored, reproducible gestures
  • ✓ Fall back to fingerspelling explicitly when a gesture is not available
  • ✓ Work with core functionality even without internet

MIRA does not

  • ✕ Show captions or subtitles — MIRA produces sign language itself
  • ✕ Use cameras to recognize or guess gestures
  • ✕ Replace human interpreters — MIRA is a tool, not a substitute
  • ✕ Generate probabilistic or unpredictable motion — every sign is traceable
  • ✕ Collect visual or biometric data of any kind

The Process

How MIRA Works

MIRA follows a deterministic pipeline. Every output is traceable from input to sign.

Step 1

Receive Input

Voice or text is received. If voice, it is first converted to text.

Step 2

Restructure to Gloss

Language is restructured into sign-friendly gloss notation — meaning over words.

Step 3

Map to Gestures

Each gloss token maps to a pre-authored, encoded gesture. Missing signs fall back to fingerspelling.

Step 4

Avatar Signs

The 3D avatar performs the sign sequence. Every motion is deterministic and reproducible.

Input → Text → Gloss → Gesture → Sign

Every output is traceable. "AI decided it" is not an acceptable explanation.

Principles

Why MIRA Is Built This Way

Core Principle

Sign Language First

Spoken language is input, not authority. MIRA restructures meaning the way a Deaf signer would naturally express it — not the way a hearing person speaks.

Non-negotiable

Accuracy Is Safety

Accuracy is not a quality metric — it is a safety constraint. "Mostly right" is treated as wrong. Incorrect sign language is considered harmful output.

By Design

Deterministic, Not Probabilistic

MIRA does not guess. Every sign is pre-authored, encoded, reproducible, and explainable. If the system cannot explain why a sign was produced, it does not produce it.

Accountability

The Deaf Community Holds Authority

Correctness is validated by Deaf community consensus first, linguistic experts second, established corpora third. Internal opinion is last.

If the Deaf community rejects something, MIRA is wrong — even if metrics say otherwise.
